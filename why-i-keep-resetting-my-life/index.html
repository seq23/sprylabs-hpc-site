Why do I keep starting over?

Because restarting gives you the feeling of control without the risk of execution. A new plan feels clean. Execution feels exposed.

The fix is not a better plan. It’s a recovery rule: after a miss, you are only allowed to do one small re-entry action (20–30 minutes). No redesign for 48 hours.

Why do I fall off after a good week?

Week one is novelty + relief. Week two is repetition. If your system depends on the “new plan high,” it collapses when repetition shows up.

Design for boring. Same loop, smaller scope on low days.

Why do I sabotage progress when it starts working?

Progress raises stakes. When stakes rise, your brain tries to protect you by lowering exposure (procrastination, distraction, perfectionism).

Make progress low-drama. Shrink outputs, keep structure.

What do I do after I miss a day?

Do not catch up. Do not punish yourself. Pick one lane, do one controlled block, stop. The goal is continuity, not redemption.

How do I stop redesigning my system every week?

Install a rule: redesign is only allowed during a scheduled review window. Outside that window you execute the smallest next step.

What’s the simplest way to rebuild momentum?

One proof of execution today. That’s it. Momentum is regained by action, not planning.

The one rule that fixes most of this

Stop trying to fix your whole life in one day. Build a Minimum Viable Day you can execute even at 60% energy. Then protect continuity.
