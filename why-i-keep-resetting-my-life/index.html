30-Second Answer

Resetting is a coping strategy: when life feels messy, you try to “wipe the slate” with a new plan. But resets kill compounding. The fix is to keep one tiny continuity line alive through chaos.

60-Second Procedure (Use This Today)

  • Pick ONE continuity action for today (20–30 minutes).
  • No backlog. No catch-up. No replatforming your life.
  • Do it now, not after planning.
  • Mark DONE.
  • Tomorrow: continue from today instead of restarting.

Why this keeps happening

  • You’re using “fresh starts” to manage anxiety.
  • You don’t trust yourself after a miss, so you reboot the whole system.
  • Your plan is too brittle — it breaks instead of bending.

FAQ

How do I stop rebuilding everything?
Replace resets with recovery: one controlled action today, then continue tomorrow. No system redesign in the moment.
What if I’m in a slump?
Shrink scope to the minimum and keep the procedure. Consistency is the only goal.
Do I need motivation to continue?
No. You need rules that run when motivation is absent.
