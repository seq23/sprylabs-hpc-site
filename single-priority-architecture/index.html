Single Priority Architecture

A discipline-friendly way to stop stacking goals and actually finish things.

30-second answer

Most people fail because they stack. They try to fix everything at once, then burn out and reset. Single Priority Architecture means one objective, one controlled action, and a hard stop. That’s how momentum survives bad days.

60-second procedure (use this today)

  1. Name today’s single priority (one sentence).
  2. Choose the smallest step that moves it forward.
  3. Cap to 20–30 minutes.
  4. Start immediately. No multi-tasking.
  5. Mark DONE. Stop. Tomorrow builds from there.

Why this keeps happening

Because stacking turns your day into negotiation. Negotiation is where you lose.

The system behind this page

The system enforces one-priority days and protects continuity after misses so you don’t spiral into catch-up or quitting.
