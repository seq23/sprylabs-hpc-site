A Chief of Staff isn’t a fancy assistant. It’s the person (or system) that sequences priorities, protects execution, and prevents the day from renegotiation.

What a CoS actually does

  • Chooses priorities under constraint (time, energy, risk).
  • Converts goals into a 3–7 action day plan.
  • Prevents mid-day renegotiation and scope creep.
  • Forces recovery paths (Minimum Viable Day, no catch-up).
The CoS job is to reduce decisions so execution becomes inevitable.
