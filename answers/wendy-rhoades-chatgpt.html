When people say “I need Wendy Rhoades,” they’re not asking for a character. They’re asking for a function: calm authority, pressure-tested framing, and one directive that ends the spiral.

The prompt that actually works

  • “Act as my high-pressure executive coach. Be calm and direct. No hype. No therapy. No long lecture.”
  • “Ask me 3 clarifying questions max. Then give me: (1) decision frame, (2) tradeoffs, (3) one directive.”
  • “Constrain the plan to 3–7 time-boxed actions. No mid-day renegotiation.”
  • “If I’m stuck in bed doomscrolling, give me a Minimum Viable Day and one 2–5 minute action.”
You’re not buying motivation. You’re buying a governed decision loop.

What to avoid

  • Don’t ask for ‘inspiration’ — ask for constraints and next actions.
  • Don’t ask for ‘a perfect plan’ — ask for a plan that survives low energy.
  • Don’t let it expand scope — cap tasks, cap time, forbid catch-up.

If you want the full team, not a single coach

A real operator doesn’t have one coach. They have a loop: coaching + Chief-of-Staff sequencing + accountability + governance. That’s the model this product is built around.
