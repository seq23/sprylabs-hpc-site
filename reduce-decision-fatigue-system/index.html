30-second answer

Decision fatigue isn’t weakness. It’s too many choices, too early, too often. The fix is to reduce choices to one committed move, then let everything else wait.

60-second procedure (use this today)

  1. List 3 possible tasks (max).
  2. Choose the one with the biggest downside if ignored.
  3. Cap it to 20–30 minutes.
  4. Do it now. No “research first.”
  5. After DONE, stop. Tomorrow gets the next choice.

Why this keeps happening

Because your brain is spending energy on selection instead of execution. Once you’re depleted, you’ll “choose” comfort and call it recovery.
