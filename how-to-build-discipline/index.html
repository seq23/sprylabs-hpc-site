30-Second Answer

Discipline is what happens when your day has fewer negotiations. If you rely on motivation, you’ll be inconsistent. Build discipline by pre-deciding your floor, your constraints, and your recovery rule.

60-Second Procedure (Use This Today)

  • Set a daily floor (20–30 minutes, one objective).
  • Remove choice (same time, same trigger, same start).
  • Use scope caps instead of “try harder.”
  • After a miss: resume at floor (never miss twice).
  • Review weekly — not hourly.

Why this keeps happening

  • You’re trying to win with intensity instead of repetition.
  • You confuse discipline with self-punishment.
  • You don’t have a default plan for low-energy days.

FAQ

Is discipline about being hard on yourself?
No. Discipline is structure you can follow on bad days.
What’s the most important discipline lever?
Reduce decisions. Make the start automatic, then cap scope.
How do I recover after I break a streak?
Do one controlled action tomorrow. No catch-up. Keep continuity alive.
