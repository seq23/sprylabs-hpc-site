30-second answer

If your tasks live in 6 places, your brain becomes the integration layer. That’s why you feel overloaded. You need one daily list, one priority, and one execution window—not another tool migration.

60-second procedure (use this today)

  1. Pick ONE “daily list” (a note, doc, or single app).
  2. Do a 2-minute sweep: dump tasks into that one list.
  3. Choose ONE task to execute today.
  4. Cap scope (20–30 minutes) and start.
  5. After DONE, stop. Repeat tomorrow.

Why this keeps happening

Because fragmentation creates constant background anxiety: “Did I forget something?” That anxiety pushes you into planning loops.
