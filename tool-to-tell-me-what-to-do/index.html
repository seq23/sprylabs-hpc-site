Tool to Tell Me What to Do

A simple way to decide what matters today—without overthinking or building a new planner.

30-second answer

You don’t need a better planner. You need a daily enforcement layer. When you’re tired or overwhelmed, your brain keeps re-deciding. This replaces the daily debate with one small, locked instruction you can follow.

60-second procedure (use this today)

  1. Write a single sentence: “Today I will do ___.”
  2. Pick the smallest real step (20–30 minutes). No planning.
  3. Remove everything else for today. One priority only.
  4. Start immediately. Set a timer.
  5. When the timer ends: mark DONE and stop. No adding.

Why this keeps happening

Because “deciding” is the hidden workload. If you have to choose from 30 tasks every morning, you’ll default to avoidance, doomscrolling, or reorganizing.
